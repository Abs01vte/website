Return of the Recurve

Knee-deep in watery mud I wade Stride after stride dragging me from the glade Dangling vines dropped by my blade Animals scurrying away from the raid Stone upon stone demarking the shoreline A sight, like travelling the desert- and finding a shrine I slump upon her shores and feel just fine As if I'm no longer fastened to the sea by twine And from this new shore I disembark The difference in energy exerted stark My way out lead by the song of a lark And right into a public park Once again among the lives of the civil Their happiness buoyed by a white pill Once the means for all those mentally ill To shirk their bonds and do what they will And I feel like I have never left the wild.
