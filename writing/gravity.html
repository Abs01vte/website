Gravity

Ribbons of smoke drifting along gentle humps

Chair, body, soul rattling from the bass

With each careful twist, you tune the music

But I didn’t even realize it sounded wrong

Focus contorting your eyes 

Furrowing your brows

And all I can think about is pressing

Your pursed lips against mine

Cupping your cheeks and admiring your eyes

Just to draw your attention to me

Even if the times when you’re in the zone

Are some of the most heart-melting to me


Oh gravity, spawn of Newton

Such laws enfolding even flesh and blood

For your soul’s gravity grabs 

And doesn’t let go

Even if I wanted to

    Which I don’t

But if I did, it’d be

More impossible to escape than 

The most intricate cage

A room made of the most indestructible material

The event horizon of a black hole

    Yet still, not apt enough comparisons


Tell me why it is

That when I look upon you

It always seems like the setting sun

Is illuminating your hair from behind

Just like in medieval paintings

-except I don’t vomit seeing you-

Making me think you’re a Valkyrie

Here to escort me off to the afterlife

But instead you usher me, facefirst

Into the world of superlife 

With sunshine smiles 

And a reassuring kiss
